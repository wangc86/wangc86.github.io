Short Bio

Starting in August 2019, I serve as an assistant professor at the Department of Computer Science and Information Engineering, National Taiwan Normal University. I am interested in timely, dependable, and efficient networked software systems research. In 2014-2019, I worked with Dr. Chris Gill and Dr. Chenyang Lu in Cyber-Physical Systems Laboratory (CPSL). In 2012-2013, I worked with Dr. Jon Turner in Applied Research Laboratory (ARL). In 2009-2010 and 2011-2012, I worked with Dr. Kuo-Feng Ssu in Dependable Computing Laboratory (DCL).

Research Projects

Fault-Tolerant Real-Time Messaging for Edge Computing (2017-2019)

Meeting subscribers’ heterogeneous requirements of message-loss tolerance and urgency.

Cyber-Physical Event Processing Middleware (CPEP) (2015-2018)

Enforcing both application-specific latency requirement and event’s temporal validity.

Safety Software Systems Design for Self-Driving Cars (2014-2015)

A collaborative research effort with General Motors, Inc. and University of Pennsylvania, USA.

Networked Virtual Worlds (2012-2013)

A scalable networking architecture to allow effective virtual interactions.

Scheduling Wireless Sensor Networks (2009-2010)

Leveraging graph theory for low-latency and adaptive wireless transmissions.
